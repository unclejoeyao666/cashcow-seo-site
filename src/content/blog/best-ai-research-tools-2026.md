### 7. Scite — Best for Citation Analysis

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can AI research tools replace a research assistant?**
For most tasks — yes. AI tools excel at literature discovery, synthesis, and first-draft writing. Human researchers still add value in experimental design, qualitative judgment, and domain expertise that requires years of field experience.

**Is Claude Pro better than ChatGPT for research?**
Yes, significantly. Claude Pro's 200K context window lets it process entire papers and books. It's also less prone to confident hallucination on technical topics. For academic or professional research, Claude Pro is the clear choice.

**Are these AI research tools allowed in academic institutions?**
Policies vary widely. Most institutions allow AI for literature search and synthesis, with disclosure requirements. Many prohibit AI-written text without attribution. Always check your institution's AI policy before submitting work.

**Which is better for systematic reviews: Elicit or Consensus?**
Elicit is purpose-built for systematic reviews with automated screening and data extraction tables. Consensus is better for quick literature discovery. Serious systematic reviewers need Elicit; casual researchers are fine with Consensus.

**What's the best free AI research tool?**
Consensus (free tier) + NotebookLM (free Google account) + Zotero (always free) = a surprisingly capable free research stack for students.

**Can these tools access paywalled research papers?**
Elicit and Scite have partial access to paywalled content through institutional partnerships. Perplexity Pro accesses some premium sources. For comprehensive paywalled access, you need your institution's library subscriptions. Tools like Unpaywall browser extension help find legal free versions.
