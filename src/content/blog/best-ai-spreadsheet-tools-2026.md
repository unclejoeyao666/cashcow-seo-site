# Best AI Spreadsheet Tools 2026

Spreadsheets remain the backbone of business — but writing formulas, cleaning data, and building reports manually is painful. AI spreadsheet tools automate the grunt work so you can focus on decisions.

Here are the **5 best AI spreadsheet tools** in 2026.

## 1. Rows — Best AI-Native Spreadsheet

**Pricing:** Free · Pro $59/mo · Team $249/mo

Rows is a spreadsheet built from the ground up with AI. Chat with your data, auto-generate charts, pull live data from APIs, and share interactive reports — all without leaving the sheet.

**Pros:**
- Natural language queries ("What was our best month?")
- Built-in integrations (Stripe, HubSpot, GA4, social media)
- Auto-generate charts and summaries
- Beautiful shareable dashboards
- Generous free tier (100 AI credits/mo)

**Cons:**
- Not Excel/Sheets compatible (different format)
- Learning curve for power features
- Team plan is pricey

**Best for:** Startups, marketers, ops teams who want a modern spreadsheet

[Try Rows →](https://rows.com/?ref=cashcow)

## 2. SheetAI — Best Google Sheets Add-on

**Pricing:** Free trial · $5.99/mo

SheetAI adds AI functions directly inside Google Sheets. Use formulas like `=AI("Categorize this expense")` or `=TRANSLATE(A1, "French")` right in your cells.

**Pros:**
- Works inside Google Sheets (no switching)
- Custom AI formulas in cells
- Bulk operations (categorize, extract, translate)
- Very affordable
- Easy to install

**Cons:**
- Dependent on Google Sheets
- Rate limits on free tier
- No standalone app

**Best for:** Google Sheets power users who want AI without leaving their workflow

[Try SheetAI →](https://sheetai.app/?ref=cashcow)

---

## 3. Arcwise — Best AI Copilot for Google Sheets

**Pricing:** Free · Pro $10/mo · Team $20/mo

Arcwise is a Google Sheets sidebar that acts as a copilot. Ask it to write formulas, explain existing ones, clean data, or suggest analyses — all in conversational language.

**Pros:**
- Context-aware (understands your full sheet)
- Writes and explains complex formulas
- Data cleaning and deduplication
- Integrates with existing Sheets
- Free tier is quite usable

**Cons:**
- Google Sheets only (no Excel)
- Needs access to your sheet data
- Complex multi-sheet operations can be slow

**Best for:** Analysts and ops people working in Google Sheets

[Try Arcwise →](https://arcwise.app/?ref=cashcow)

## 4. Equals — Best for SaaS/Startup Reporting

**Pricing:** Free · Starter $49/mo · Pro $99/mo

Equals connects directly to your databases (Postgres, Snowflake, BigQuery) and SaaS tools, combining spreadsheet flexibility with SQL power and AI analysis.

**Pros:**
- SQL + spreadsheet hybrid
- Live database connections
- AI-generated insights and anomaly detection
- Version history and collaboration
- Scheduled reports and alerts

**Cons:**
- Expensive for solo users
- Overkill for simple spreadsheet tasks
- Requires database/SQL knowledge for full value

**Best for:** SaaS teams, finance ops, data-driven startups

[Try Equals →](https://equals.com/?ref=cashcow)

---

## 5. Julius — Best for Data Analysis & Visualization

**Pricing:** Free tier · Pro $20/mo · Teams $40/mo

Julius isn't a traditional spreadsheet — it's an AI data analyst. Upload a CSV, Excel, or connect a database, then ask questions in plain English. It writes Python/SQL behind the scenes.

**Pros:**
- Upload any data format (CSV, Excel, databases)
- Natural language → Python/SQL analysis
- Auto-generated charts and visualizations
- Export to PDF/PPT reports
- Handles complex statistical analysis

**Cons:**
- Not a real-time spreadsheet (analysis tool)
- Can be slow on large datasets
- Occasional hallucinations in analysis

**Best for:** Researchers, consultants, anyone analyzing datasets

[Try Julius →](https://julius.ai/?ref=cashcow)
