So you're ready to launch — or level up — your podcast in 2026, and you've narrowed it down to three heavyweights: **Descript**, **Riverside**, and **Squadcast**. Smart move. Here's the thing: each of these tools has evolved *massively* over the past year. Descript doubled down on AI editing magic. Riverside went all-in on studio-grade remote recording. And Squadcast — now under the Descript umbrella — carved out its own niche for reliability-first podcasters.

## Quick Overview: What Each Tool Does Best

Before we get into the weeds, here's the 30-second version:

- **Descript** = Best for creators who want an all-in-one recording + editing + publishing workflow powered by AI
- **Riverside** = Best for podcasters and video creators who need studio-quality remote recordings with zero compromise
- **Squadcast** = Best for interview-heavy podcasters who prioritize reliability and simplicity above everything else

Now let's unpack each one.

## Descript: The AI-First Editing Powerhouse

Descript started as a transcription tool and has evolved into something much bigger — a full creative suite where you edit audio and video by editing text. Yes, literally. You delete a word from the transcript, and the audio clip disappears too.

### Key Features (2026)

- **AI-powered text-based editing** — Edit your podcast like a Google Doc
- **Studio Sound** — One-click AI enhancement that removes background noise, fixes room echo, and normalizes levels
- **AI Voices & Overdub** — Clone your voice and fix flubbed lines without re-recording
- **Filler word removal** — Automatically strip "um," "uh," "like," and "you know" across entire episodes
- **Screen recording** — Built-in for tutorial-style content
- **AI Summaries & Show Notes** — Generate episode descriptions, chapters, and social clips automatically
- **Multi-track recording** — Record remote guests directly in Descript
- **Publishing** — Distribute directly to Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and more

### Pros
✅ Unmatched AI editing capabilities — nothing else comes close to text-based editing  
✅ All-in-one platform (record, edit, publish, repurpose)  
✅ Filler word and silence removal saves *hours* per episode  
✅ Overdub voice cloning is genuinely impressive  
✅ Great for solo creators and small teams  
✅ Excellent template library for social clips  

### Cons
❌ Learning curve — the text-based editing paradigm takes adjustment  
❌ Recording quality is good but not as pristine as Riverside's local recording  
❌ Can feel sluggish with very long recordings (2+ hours)  
❌ Advanced features locked behind higher tiers  
❌ Squadcast integration, while improving, still feels like a separate product  

### Who Should Use Descript?

If you're a solo podcaster or content creator who wants to do *everything* in one place — record, edit, transcribe, create clips, publish — Descript is hard to beat. It's especially powerful if you're also creating video content and want AI to handle the tedious editing work. ## Riverside: Studio-Quality Remote Recording

Riverside has one obsession: making remote recordings sound and look like everyone was in the same room. Their secret sauce? **Local recording**. Instead of relying on your internet connection (which will inevitably hiccup), Riverside records each participant's audio and video locally at full quality, then uploads the tracks after the session.

### Key Features (2026)

- **Local HD recording** — Up to 4K video and 48kHz WAV audio, recorded locally on each participant's device
- **AI Transcription** — Real-time transcripts in 100+ languages
- **AI-powered Magic Clips** — Automatically identifies the best moments and generates social-ready clips
- **Separate tracks** — Isolated audio and video tracks for every participant
- **Producer mode** — Have a producer join without being recorded
- **Live streaming** — Simulcast to YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitch, and more
- **AI Show Notes** — Auto-generated summaries, timestamps, and chapters
- **Text-based editing** — Yes, Riverside added this too (though it's not as mature as Descript's)
- **Mobile recording** — iOS and Android apps for recording on the go

### Pros
✅ Best-in-class recording quality — period  
✅ Local recording means internet issues don't ruin your episode  
✅ Separate tracks give you full control in post-production  
✅ Magic Clips AI is genuinely useful for social media repurposing  
✅ Live streaming integration is a nice bonus  
✅ Excellent mobile apps  
✅ Producer mode is great for managed podcasts  

### Cons
❌ Editing capabilities are improving but still behind Descript  
❌ No built-in podcast hosting/distribution  
❌ Can be pricey for solo creators on higher tiers  
❌ Text-based editor is relatively new and less polished  
❌ File uploads can take time after long recording sessions  

### Who Should Use Riverside?

If recording quality is your #1 priority — especially for video podcasts — Riverside is the gold standard. It's also the best choice if you regularly record with remote guests and can't afford to lose a session to a bad internet connection. Video podcasters looking to repurpose content into social clips will love the Magic Clips feature. Speaking of video, you might also want to explore the [best AI video editing tools for 2026](/cashcow-seo-site/blog/best-ai-video-editing-tools-2026) to complete your production stack.

## Squadcast: Reliability You Can Count On

Squadcast was acquired by Descript in 2022, but it still operates as its own product with a clear mission: **make remote podcast recording as reliable as possible**. No dropped connections. No lost recordings. No drama.

### Key Features (2026)

- **Progressive upload technology** — Audio is uploaded in real-time chunks, so even if someone disconnects, you never lose more than a few seconds
- **Separate track recording** — Individual WAV files for each participant
- **Video recording** — Up to 1080p with separate video tracks
- **Green Room** — Participants can test their setup before going live
- **Dolby integration** — AI-powered audio enhancement through Dolby.io
- **Session management** — Easy scheduling and guest invitation system
- **Recording backup** — Redundant cloud backup for every session
- **Descript integration** — One-click export to Descript for editing

### Pros
✅ Rock-solid reliability — progressive uploads mean you almost never lose audio  
✅ Dead simple interface — guests can join with zero technical knowledge  
✅ Excellent audio quality with Dolby enhancement  
✅ Green Room reduces tech-check hassles  
✅ Seamless Descript integration for editing  
✅ Great for teams and networks with multiple shows  

### Cons
❌ Fewer AI features compared to Descript and Riverside  
❌ No built-in text-based editing  
❌ Video limited to 1080p (Riverside offers 4K)  
❌ No live streaming capability  
❌ No built-in social clip creation  
❌ Uncertain long-term roadmap given Descript ownership  

### Who Should Use Squadcast?

If you run an interview-heavy podcast and your biggest fear is losing a recording, Squadcast's progressive upload technology is your insurance policy. It's also great if you want a simple, no-fuss recording experience combined with Descript's editing power. ## Real-World Workflow Comparison

Let's walk through a typical podcast episode workflow with each tool:

### Descript Workflow
1. Record in Descript (or import audio) → 2. AI transcribes automatically → 3. Remove filler words with one click → 4. Edit by deleting/rearranging text → 5. Apply Studio Sound → 6. Generate AI show notes and chapters → 7. Create social clips from templates → 8. Publish directly to podcast platforms

**Time to publish:** ~45 minutes for a 1-hour episode

### Riverside Workflow
1. Schedule session and invite guests → 2. Record in Riverside (local recording) → 3. Download separate tracks → 4. Use Magic Clips for social content → 5. Export to your preferred editor (or use built-in text editor) → 6. Publish via your hosting platform

**Time to publish:** ~60-90 minutes (depends on your editing tool)

### Squadcast Workflow
1. Schedule session via Squadcast → 2. Guests join Green Room for setup check → 3. Record (progressive upload protects your files) → 4. Export separate tracks → 5. One-click send to Descript for editing → 6. Edit and publish via Descript

**Time to publish:** ~60-75 minutes (including Descript editing)

## The Final Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

After extensive testing, here's my honest recommendation:

### 🏆 Choose Descript if...
- You want the fastest path from recording to published episode

- You're a solo creator or small team who needs an all-in-one solution

- You produce video podcasts and need 4K

- You need mobile recording capabilities

### 🏆 Choose Squadcast if...
- You've ever lost a recording and swore "never again"
- You record lots of interviews and need bulletproof reliability
- Your guests are non-technical and need the simplest possible experience
- You're already using (or plan to use) Descript for editing
- You want an affordable, focused recording tool without feature bloat

### My Personal Pick?

For most podcasters in 2026, I'd recommend **Riverside Pro** as the starting point. The recording quality is unmatched, the Magic Clips feature saves enormous time on social content, and at $29/month it's incredibly competitive. Pair it with a solid editing tool and you've got a professional-grade setup.

And don't sleep on **Squadcast** — especially if reliability is your top concern. Losing a great interview because of a technical glitch is a nightmare that Squadcast was specifically built to prevent.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use Descript and Riverside together?**
Absolutely. Many podcasters record in Riverside for maximum quality, then import their tracks into Descript for AI-powered editing. It's actually a fantastic combo.

**Is Squadcast being shut down since Descript acquired it?**
No — as of 2026, Squadcast continues to operate as a standalone product. Descript has been integrating features between the two platforms, but Squadcast maintains its own identity and development roadmap.

**Which tool has the best AI transcription?**
All three offer excellent AI transcription accuracy (95%+ for English). Descript edges ahead slightly for multi-speaker identification. Riverside supports the most languages (100+).

**Do guests need to install anything?**
No — all three tools work in the browser. Guests just click a link and join. Riverside and Squadcast do recommend Chrome for the best experience.

**Can I switch between tools easily?**
Yes. Since all three export standard audio/video formats, you're never locked in. Your content is always portable.
