## Key Features in 2026

### 1. Model V7 — The Quality Leap

Midjourney V7 (released late 2025) is a dramatic improvement over V6:

- **Better prompt adherence** — complex multi-subject scenes now render accurately
- **Photorealism** — skin texture, hair detail, and lighting are near-indistinguishable from photography
- **Consistent characters** — `--cref` (character reference) lets you maintain the same character across images
- **Style references** — `--sref` lets you clone a visual style from any reference image

### 2. Web Interface

After years of Discord-only access, Midjourney's web app is finally solid:

- Prompt history and image management
- Remix and variation controls
- Collections and organization
- Shareable galleries

Still not as powerful as the Discord workflow for prompt iteration, but great for beginners.

### 3. Inpainting & Outpainting

The **Vary (Region)** feature lets you select and regenerate specific parts of an image — essential for fixing hands, faces, or backgrounds without redoing the entire generation.

**Outpainting** extends images beyond their original borders, useful for creating landscape headers from portrait shots.

### 4. Video Generation (Alpha)

Midjourney added video generation in alpha — animate existing images with subtle motion. Still limited compared to dedicated tools like Runway or Kling, but promising.

## Tips for Getting the Best Results

1. **Use style references (`--sref`)** — paste any image URL to match its aesthetic
2. **Specify aspect ratios** — `--ar 16:9` for YouTube thumbnails, `--ar 9:16` for Instagram Stories
3. **Use `--stylize`** — `--stylize 0` for literal prompts, `--stylize 1000` for maximum artistic interpretation
4. **Negative prompts** — `--no blurry, watermark, text` removes common issues
5. **Start with community prompts** — browse `/explore` in the Midjourney web app for proven prompt formulas
