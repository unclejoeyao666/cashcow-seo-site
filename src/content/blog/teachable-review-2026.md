# Teachable Review 2026: Is It Still the Best Online Course Platform?

If you're looking to sell online courses, Teachable has been one of the go-to platforms since 2014. But with so many competitors entering the space, is it still worth it in 2026?

I've spent considerable time testing Teachable's features, comparing pricing plans, and analyzing what types of creators actually succeed on the platform. Here's my honest take.

---

## What Is Teachable?

Teachable is an all-in-one platform that lets creators sell **online courses, digital downloads, memberships, and coaching sessions**. It handles everything from hosting to payment processing to student management.

**Key stats:**
- 100,000+ creators on the platform
- $1B+ in creator revenue processed
- Used by creators in 100+ countries

## Top Features

### 1. Course Builder
Teachable's drag-and-drop course builder is genuinely easy to use. You can upload:
- Video (hosted on Teachable's servers)
- PDFs, audio, images
- Live sessions via Zoom integration
- Quizzes and assessments

### 2. Payment Processing
Teachable handles payments natively in 130+ currencies. They support:
- Credit/debit cards
- PayPal
- Buy now, pay later (Afterpay, Klarna)
- One-time, subscription, and payment plan options

### 3. AI Features (New in 2025-2026)
Teachable has rolled out AI tools for:
- Auto-generating course outlines
- Creating quiz questions from content
- Writing course descriptions and sales page copy

This is a significant time-saver for new creators.

### 4. Affiliate Management
Teachable has a built-in affiliate program feature (Pro plan+) that lets you recruit your own affiliates to promote your courses. This is separate from the Teachable affiliate program where *you* promote Teachable.

## Teachable vs. Thinkific

Both platforms are popular, but there are key differences:

| Feature | Teachable | Thinkific |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Free Plan | ✅ (10% fee) | ✅ (0% fee) |
| Transaction Fees | 0–10% | 0% (all paid plans) |
| AI Features | ✅ Strong | ⚠️ Limited |
| Built-in Affiliate Tools | Pro+ only | ✅ All plans |
| Custom Domain | Pro+ | Free plan |
| Best For | Creators wanting simplicity | Creators wanting more control |

**Verdict:** Thinkific's free plan is more generous (no transaction fees), but Teachable's AI tools and UI are more polished. If you're just starting out, test both.

## Who Should Use Teachable?

**Teachable is great for:**
- ✅ First-time course creators who want simplicity
- ✅ Creators who want AI help building course content
- ✅ Anyone selling coaching + courses together
- ✅ Creators in the $0–$10K/month revenue range

**Teachable might not be the best if:**
- ❌ You need a full marketing funnel platform (use Kajabi)
- ❌ You want zero transaction fees on a free plan (use Thinkific)
- ❌ You're building a large community (use Skool or Circle)

## Pros and Cons

### ✅ Pros
- Very user-friendly, low learning curve
- Strong AI content generation tools
- Handles all payment processing (no Stripe setup needed)
- Good student analytics
- Native mobile app for students

### ❌ Cons
- Transaction fees on Basic plan (5%) eat into revenue
- Limited marketing/funnel capabilities vs Kajabi
- 30-day affiliate cookie window (industry average is 60-90 days)
- No built-in community features

---

## Final Verdict

**Teachable scores 8/10 for course creators starting out.** It hits the sweet spot between ease of use and features. The AI tools are genuinely useful, and the payment infrastructure is solid.

For most creators selling their first 1-3 courses, Teachable Pro ($119/month) provides the best ROI. Once you're generating $5K+/month, revisit Kajabi for its full business suite.
