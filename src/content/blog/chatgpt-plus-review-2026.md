## Deep Dive: Each Feature Tested

### 1. GPT-4o — The Everyday Workhorse

GPT-4o is the model you'll use most. In 2026, it's noticeably better than the GPT-4o I used in 2024:

**What it's good at:**

### 2. o3 Reasoning Model — The Problem Solver

o3 is where ChatGPT Plus gets genuinely premium. It's not just a smarter language model — it's a reasoning engine that "thinks through" problems before answering.

**What it's good at:**
- Complex math and logic problems
- Multi-step planning and strategy
- Debugging tricky code
- Legal/financial reasoning tasks
- Research synthesis across conflicting sources

**Real-world test:** I gave o3 a problem I'd been stuck on for weeks: optimizing a pricing model for a SaaS product with complex tier structures and churn variables. It spent ~45 seconds "thinking," then delivered a 1,200-word analysis with a recommended model, edge cases, and a sensitivity analysis.

GPT-4o gave me a decent answer in 10 seconds. o3's answer was objectively better — more thorough, more mathematically rigorous, and caught two errors in my assumptions.

**Verdict:** 9.5/10 for complex work. Access to o3 alone may justify the $20 subscription for professionals.

### 3. Advanced Voice Mode — The AI Assistant You Imagined

Advanced Voice Mode (AVM) launched in late 2024 and has matured considerably. You can have natural, real-time voice conversations with GPT-4o — not just speech-to-text, but actual voice-based reasoning.

**What it's good at:**
- Hands-free brainstorming while walking/commuting
- Language practice (it corrects your grammar in real-time)
- Interview prep (surprisingly effective)
- Reading and explaining documents aloud

**Where it falls short:**
- Can't access the web or files during voice sessions
- Occasionally misunderstands complex technical terms
- No memory of previous voice sessions (yet)

**Real-world test:** I used AVM for 30 minutes of German language practice. It corrected my grammar, adjusted its pace when I asked, and even played a "shopkeeper" role-play scenario to make the practice contextual. Duolingo can't do this.

**Verdict:** 8/10. Genuinely useful, especially for language learning and audio content creation.
