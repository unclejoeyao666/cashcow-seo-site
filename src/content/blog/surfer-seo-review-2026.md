## Content Editor: The Core Product

The Content Editor is where Surfer earns its reputation. Write or paste content, enter your target keyword, and Surfer scores your article from 0–100 based on:

- **Word count** vs. top competitors
- **NLP keyword usage** (terms that semantically relate to your topic)
- **Headings structure** and density
- **Paragraph count** and structure patterns
- **Image count**

**What makes it actually useful:**

The keyword suggestions aren't random — they're extracted from the actual top 10–20 ranking pages for your query. When Surfer says "include the term 'technical SEO audit checklist' 3 times," that's because it appears frequently in pages that Google already trusts.

**Honest limitations:**

- The score is a guideline, not gospel. Stuffing keywords to hit 90+ can hurt readability
- It doesn't account for E-E-A-T signals (author credibility, brand authority)
- New sites still need backlinks; Surfer can't replace domain authority

**Verdict on Content Editor: 9/10** — Best-in-class for on-page optimization.

## SERP Analyzer: Deep Competitive Intelligence

Enter any keyword and get a breakdown of:

- Average word count, headings, images of top 10 results

- Backlink profiles of top pages

The correlation charts are genuinely insightful. For some queries, word count barely matters; for others, it's the strongest predictor. Surfer shows you the actual data.

The Keyword Research tool groups keywords into clusters and suggests content gaps. The **Topical Map** feature (Scale plan and above) visualizes an entire topic authority strategy:

- Enter a main topic (e.g., "email marketing")
- Get 50–200 subtopics organized into clusters
- See which clusters you've covered vs. gaps

For content teams building authority in a niche, this feature alone can justify the Scale plan.

**Verdict: 8/10** — Excellent for content strategy; not a replacement for Ahrefs/Semrush for technical keyword research.

The Audit analyzes URLs you've already published and tells you:

**ROI use case:** If you have 50 existing blog posts that are "almost ranking" (positions 8–20), running them through Surfer Audit and updating them can move multiple pages to Page 1 with a few hours of work.

**Verdict: 9/10** — Possibly the highest-ROI feature for established sites.
